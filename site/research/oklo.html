The story vs the reality

The story: Oklo builds "Aurora" microreactors and sells the electricity on 20-year contracts — clean 24/7 baseload for AI data centers, a ~14 GW pipeline, marquee names (Switch, Meta, Equinix), and Sam Altman's halo. The reality: it's pre-revenue, has no operating reactor, holds no NRC license yet (its 2022 application was denied), and first power is ~late 2027–2028. Most of that pipeline is non-binding LOIs, not orders.

The real bull case (it's not nothing)

  • Genuine first-mover lead on a non-light-water license, deep DOE/Idaho National Lab ties, and a fuel-recycling angle.
  • Well-funded. A ~$1.2B raise pushed cash to $2.54B — multi-year runway, low near-term bankruptcy risk.
  • Regulatory momentum in 2026 — passed the NRC readiness assessment, got Principal Design Criteria approved on an accelerated schedule.
  • Right theme. If AI needs clean 24/7 baseload at scale, nuclear is the honest answer — and Oklo is a pure-play.

Why it's a "no" for our style right now

  • It's in a downtrend. Down ~73% from its peak, trading below both its 50-day (~$60) and 200-day (~$86). Trend-followers don't buy broken charts.
  • Binary, not trend. The next moves hinge on regulatory events (NRC license, criticality dates) — that's coin-flip speculation, not a thesis you can trail a stop under.
  • Active short thesis. Kerrisdale argues fuel costs are lowballed ~5x and it needs ~$2.7B more; Forbes called it a "$14B paper reactor bubble."
  • Red flags. Heavy insider selling (CEO/COO >$50M in Jan '26), an open securities-fraud investigation, and priced at ~3x NuScale — the only rival with an NRC-certified design.

Trade frame — trend-follower, weeks to 6 months

Stance

Avoid / watch. This is a speculative catalyst bet in a downtrend, not a trend trade. Wait for a base and a reclaim of the moving averages before it's even a candidate.

What would make it interesting

A confirmed NRC licensing milestone + price basing above ~$60 (reclaiming the 50-day) on volume. Then it becomes a trend candidate, not before.

If ever taken

Tiny position only (beta ~3.7, 8–12% daily swings), wide stop below the base low, sized so a total loss barely dents the sleeve.

Catalysts to watch

NRC combined-license progress · "Groves" criticality milestone · Q2 earnings (~Aug) · any LOI converting to a binding PPA.

The lesson: same theme, opposite trade

Bloom and Oklo are both "energy for AI" — and the research says treat them completely differently. Bloom is a real business with a rich, extended stock (buy pullbacks). Oklo is a pre-revenue story with a broken chart (watch, don't touch). A headline-reaction bot would lump them together as "AI power longs." Deep research is what separates the trade you want from the one that looks identical and isn't.
