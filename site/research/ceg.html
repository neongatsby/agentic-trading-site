The thesis in one breath

Constellation runs the largest US nuclear fleet (~32 GW) — exactly the clean, 24/7 baseload hyperscalers need. It's signed 20-year PPAs with Microsoft (restarting Three Mile Island) and Meta, just closed the $26.6B Calpine deal (adding gas + Texas), and unlike Bloom or Oklo it's genuinely, richly profitable. The catch: after the AI-power euphoria faded, the stock fell ~42% and is now in a clear downtrend.

The bull case (it's the strong one here)

  • Scarce, contracted assets. Signed 20-year PPAs: Microsoft/TMI 835 MW (2027 restart) and Meta/Clinton 1,121 MW — real cash flows, not LOIs.
  • Actually profitable + growing 20%+. The only one of the three energy names with real earnings, a $5B buyback, and a PTC revenue floor (~$43.75/MWh).
  • Calpine adds scale. $26.6B deal closed Jan '26 — gas, retail, Texas; guided ~20% EPS accretion in 2026.
  • Reasonably valued after the drop. ~20x forward earnings for 20%+ growth, ~50% to the average analyst target.

Why it's a WAIT, not a buy (yet)

  • The chart is broken. ~42% off its high, below both the 50-day (~$279) and 200-day (~$310), just tagged a 52-week low near $229. Trend-followers don't buy falling knives.
  • Guidance underwhelmed. FY26 EPS guide came in below consensus — the market punished it.
  • Restart timing risk. The TMI/Crane restart targets 2027 but PJM grid delays could slip it; still needs final NRC sign-off.
  • Share overhang. Calpine holders got ~14% of CEG; half the lock-up released Jun 30 — supply pressure right into the slide.

Trade frame — trend-follower, weeks to 6 months
