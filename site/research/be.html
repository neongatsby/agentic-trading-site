The thesis in one breath

AI data centers need gigawatts of power now, but grid interconnection runs 4–12 years and gas turbines are sold out through ~2027–2029. Bloom's fuel cells install in weeks (55 days for an Oracle site vs a 90-day target), run on natural gas today, and are modular from 20 MW to 500 MW+. That speed-to-power is the real edge — and hyperscalers are signing up for it.

Why it wins right now (the moat)

  • Speed. Weeks-to-power vs years for grid or turbines — the single biggest reason deals are landing.
  • Real backlog. Reported $20B+, and over half the data-center backlog is non-Oracle — demand is broadening.
  • Marquee customers. Oracle MSA up to 2.8 GW (1.2 GW contracted); AEP 1 GW; Nebius $2.6B; Brookfield partnership.
  • Scaled leader. ~5 GW/yr capacity — far ahead of fuel-cell rivals (FuelCell Energy, Plug, Doosan).

The bear case (read this twice)

  • Priced for perfection. ~112x forward earnings, ~31x sales, after +1,100% in a year. Analyst avg target (~$279) is basically at the price — no upside to consensus.
  • Not actually clean. Runs mostly on natural gas (~680–960 lb CO₂/MWh); emissions scrutiny + gas-price exposure undercut the green label.
  • Subsidy-dependent. Economics lean on the 30% fuel-cell ITC (§48E). A repeal or curtailment is a direct hit to pricing.
  • "Bubble" risk. Short-seller Jim Chanos argues AI-power scarcity is a temporary bottleneck; faster grid interconnects (FERC) could erase the scarcity premium.
  • History & dilution. 24 years without sustainable profit, ~$4B accumulated deficit, chronic share issuance, and a 2019 short report alleging aggressive accounting.

Exit (let winners run)

No fixed take-profit. Trail beneath the rising 50-day, or exit on thesis-invalidation (below) rather than a price target.

What would kill the thesis (exit triggers)

  • FY26 guidance cut or gross margin reversal below ~30%.
  • A marquee deal slips — Oracle ramp stalls or Brookfield framework fails to convert to orders.
  • ITC / §48E repeal or curtailment for fuel cells.
  • A dilutive equity raise near current levels, or an accounting/warranty red flag.
  • Broad AI-capex slowdown, or FERC-driven faster grid interconnects erasing the scarcity premium.
